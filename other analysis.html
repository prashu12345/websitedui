Total Number of Crashes by Percent of Grade

This chart displays the total number of crashes by the percent of road grade. PLUS and MINUS grades highlight uphill and downhill crash patterns, revealing that most crashes occur on low grades between 1% and 3%.

Total Number of Crashes by AADT and Direction of Curve

This scatter plot shows the relationship between Annual Average Daily Traffic (AADT) and the total number of crashes, categorized by the direction of the curve (Left or Right). Key observations:

  • Crashes tend to increase with higher AADT, but the distribution is more concentrated in the lower AADT range (below 50K).
  • Left-hand curves (in red) show a higher density of crashes compared to right-hand curves (in blue), especially at moderate traffic levels.
  • At higher AADT levels (above 150K), crashes are less frequent, indicating potential driver caution on busier roads or better infrastructure on high-traffic routes.
